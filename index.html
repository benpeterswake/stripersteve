Stripers on Lanier

Striped bass or "Stripers" (Morone saxatilis) are actively stocked in Lake Lanier to provide fishing recreation. Starting as "fingerlings" they grow on an average of 2-3 lbs per year. They range in size from a couple of pounds, up to the lake record of 48+ lbs. While catching fish in the 3-10 lbs range are common, we often catch fish in the 10-20 lbs range, with the largest ever caught on my boat going over 40 lbs. Besides being a blast to catch, Stripers also provide for very good table fare ( recipes). The fish "migrate" up and down the lake as the seasons change, depending on the water temperature and where their main forage or "bait" (blue back herring) are located. My years of experience, fishing across all the seasons, gives me the know-how to put you on to them, whenever you can go out!
